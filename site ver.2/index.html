Lab 2

─────────────

Lab 2 focuses on the utilization of GPIO ports on the STM board. By using a GPIO port I must figure out how to record the time between each button press and create an algorithm that counts the reaction time once an onboard LED is lit.

Lab 3

─────────────

Lab 3 orients itself around the implenentation and introduction of an external circuit. By using a circuit that features an EEPROM chip, my partner and I must create a program that where a user can change the time, date, and day of the week, where all forms of time increment in real time.

Lab 4

─────────────

Lab 4 features the use of an external circuit to power a 12 W fan. The STM board, unable to supply a sufficient amount of power, must be hooked up to an external circuit that can power the fan. This lab heavily emphasizes the use of ADC (Analog to Digital Conversion) and DAC (Digital to Analog Conversion).

Lab 5

─────────────

Lab 5 utilizes an external circuit to power a stepper motor. This lab delves into the physics and concepts used to develop a stepper motor i.e (Full-stepping, Half-stepping, Angular Resolution, SRM's, etc.)

H5 (Power Factor Correction)
